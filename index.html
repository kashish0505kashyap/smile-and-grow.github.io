Why smile is important?

Life

A smile expresses pleasure, sociability,happiness, joy or amusement. A smile seems to have a favorable influence upon others and makes one likable and more approachable Smiling is good for your health in a number of ways. It can reduce stress, help heart health, lower blood pressure, and boost your immune system by decreasing cortisol in the body. A simple smile, genuine or even forced, prompts the brain to produce endorphins and serotonin, causing positive emotions. Smiling not only offers a mood boost but helps our bodies release cortisol and endorphins that provide numerous health benefits, including: Reduced blood pressure, Increased endurance. It also helps in prevention as well as cure of depression and related problems. Smiling reduces stress that your body and mind feel, almost similar to getting good sleep, according to recent studies. And smiling helps to generate more positive emotions within you. That's why we often feel happier around children – they smile more. On average, they do so 400 times a day. Smiling can trick the body into helping you elevate your mood because the physical act of smiling actually activates neural messaging in your brain. scientific studies have indicated that people can derive the same amount of happiness from smiling than from physical exercise. Those who smile or laugh often will tend to be happier, more energetic, and healthier. On the other end of the spectrum, a grumpy person may have an identity connected with feeling unloved, victimized, or marginalized.A simple smile will not cost anything to you but will give a lot to you . In your world of busy schedule and tensions try to smile often , it will help you feel positive vibes.
